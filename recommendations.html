RECOMMENDATIONS

Research Recommendations:

  • Urban Air Mobility (UAM) research should be continued
    • Small aircraft that seat 6 or less may appeal to people who are worried about their safety
      • Electric vertical take-off and landing vehicles (EVTOLS)
    • Research on how the public would respond to Urban Air Mobility
  • Increasing air traffic control (ATC) effienciency should continue
    • UAM would require ATC infrastructure
    • More private chartered flights for social distancing
      • Smaller aircraft with less passengers may mean increase in air traffic
    • Research that would support this development are projects such as NextGen, ATD-2, and testing done in the Air Traffic Control Simulator
  • Research on Unmanned Aircraft System Traffic Management should continue
    • More people can stay at home and receive deliveries
    • Delivery with less contact

Guidelines For The Future:

Safety

  • In the case of a pandemic or health crisis, airlines should promote safety in airports and aircraft:
    • Information about where it has originated, how it is spread, and how to prevent the spread of the virus should be prominently displayed as soon as possible
    • Airlines should also require masks, social distancing, and sanitation once the virus is seen to be a threat
    • Flexible booking should be put in place, as it allows someone who is sick to cancel their flight and therefore, protect others from exposure
    • Travel documents should be digital in order to minimize contact between people
    • There should be protocols in place for biometric testing
    • Contact tracing should be in place to warn passengers who were in close proximity to someone who has a virus

Recommendations to Protect Economic Interests:

  • Build Customer Confidence
    • Safety guidelines help protect passengers and airline workers and improve customer confidence
    • An increase in customer confidence can lead to an increase in bookings
  • Create flexibility for aircraft usage
    • Cargo shipping has not been as affected as passenger travel
    • Strip seats from passenger planes to ship cargo
    • Integrate cargo shipping with passenger planes to establish flexibility for the future
