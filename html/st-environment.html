ENVIRONMENT

Air pollution from aviation is an important topic followed by many advocacy groups. It adversely affects human health and contributes to global greenhouse effects. During COVID, airline activity has plummeted, reducing its burden on peopleâ€™s respiratory systems and the environment.

AIRCRAFT POLLUTION IS SIGNIFICANT:

  • Aircraft pollution makes up 12% of transportation emissions
  • A single airliner creates 285 g/co2/passenger/km vs a car's 55 g/co2/passenger/km
  • Pollution concentrates around airports, for example harmful particles from LAX have been found to be spread 20 square miles in the airport's area.

COVID-19

The pandemic has led to a decrease in aircraft operations. This contributed to changes in pollution, which multiple institutions have measured. According to the University of Washington, general air pollution (PM2.5) has fluctuated during COVID-19. During a temporary stay-at-home order:

Another study by Swiss air technology company IQAir shows noticeable air quality improvement in Los Angeles. During a 3-week lockdown period in 2020:

  • PM2.5 pollution dropped 31% from last year
  • PM2.5 dropped 51% from the average of 2016-2019
